BENCHMARK VALLEY BEHAVIORAL HOME ​PROGRAM DESCRIPTION

1. Benchmark valley provides two (2), five (5) bed licensed homes located in Maricopa county. Each home has 24-hour staff supervision. Staff to resident ratio may vary based upon the needs of the residents in the home however, all staff are trained and competent in Behavioral health care, Personal care and crisis intervention. 2.Benchmark valley provides programs that empower patients to seek a bright future. We create a unique road map, or plan to guide them as they navigate the journey from crisis to wellness. Each patient is assessed, and our team creates an individualized treatment plan tailored to the needs of the patient. All treatment plans include comprehensive discharge planning. 3. Benchmark valley offers its clients training and support in developing skills in community use and living, problem solving, leisure skills and social relationships skills. All these training is provided in each home in accordance with the choice of the resident as outlined in their person centered treatment plan. 4. Licensed clinical staff-Benchmark valley utilizes a team of Licensed clinician, Registered Nurse, Behavioral Health Professional(BHP), Behavioral Health Technician(BHT), Dietitian and other contracted health agencies. 5. Treatment Plan-Treatment plan is developed by our clinician based on individual needs, and participation is required with a minimum of one hour counselling of each individual weekly and one hour in group therapy weekly. These counselling and group therapy help to address symptoms and provide support to maintain healthy goals. 6. Assistance in self -administration of medication, Medication monitoring, education and dispensing is provided by our trained personnel 7. Personal care services-Personal care services are one of the scope of services that we provide 8. Semi-private or private rooms are available. Room assignment is based upon individual needs and availability. 9. Benchmark Valley provides meals, laundry services and transportation to community resources, banks, malls, grocery store, churches, libraries, local parks just to name but a few. PROGRAM ACCESS Benchmark valley generally has no waiting list. We accept admissions 24/7

ENTRANCE/ADMISSION CRITERIA

1. Adults (male and female) 18 years and over with severe mental illness, substance abuse diagnosis, alcohol addiction, dual diagnosis or co-occurring disorders. 2. Indication that the individual can benefit from the program and its services 3. The ability to reside in an open setting 4. Compatible behavior that does not pose significant risk or danger to the well-being of self or others. 5. Completion of all financial arrangements /Authorization from the TRBHA (Tribal regional behavioral health authority), or insurance plan.

EXIT/DISCHARGE CRITERIA

1. The deterioration in an individual’s physical or psychological condition requiring specific services not available through Benchmark Valley 2. Disruptive behavior by the individual that poses significant risk or danger to the well-being of self or others 3. No longer benefits from, or needs, the program and its services 4. Loss of authorization from the payer. Inability to pay for services 5. Achieves /obtain the goals as measured by the resident’s Health plan psychiatrist, and the registered nurse and can maintain /function in a less restrictive setting. Attends to ADL’s without prompting, medication compliant and self-administered, seeks out needed services when appropriate, maintains physical and psychological functioning. 6. Self-determination of any resident who is not under court order or guardianship to terminate from the program.

THERAPEUTIC ORGANIZED ACTIVITIES AND ADDITIONAL SERVICES

Activities Programs are necessary for the overall well-being of our residents. 1. An activity program will be planned monthly 2. The activities planned will reflect the individual resident’s preferences, needs and abilities 3. A copy of the activities calendar will be retained on file on the premises for 12 months 4. The activities will be posted daily on the notice board. 5. A daily newspaper, current magazines and a variety of reading material will be available to all residents 6. Changes and substitutions will be indicated on the activity calendar 7. The BHT, BHPP, BHP on duty will implement the activities program. The activities will include but not limited to; Social/Recreation-leisure skills. 8. Treatment programs at the local Alcoholic Anonymous, Narcotics Anonymous and YMCA

SERVICES FOR RESIDENTS WITH SPECIAL NEEDS

Benchmark valley facilities are equipped with bath and shower grab bars. We are also wheel chair accessible.
